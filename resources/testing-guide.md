# Testing Your Prototype for Signal

How to test your prototype and know if you're onto something.

---

## The Core Principle

> Don't fall in love with the prototype — fall in love with learning.

A prototype is a learning tool, not a finished product. The goal is signal, not validation.

---

## What Prototypes Are Good For

- Concrete conversations ("Look at this and react")
- Finding confusion points ("What do you think this does?")
- Generating behavior questions ("Would you actually use this?")

## What Prototypes Are NOT Good For

- Measuring demand (no one has committed anything)
- Proving willingness to pay (no transaction happened)
- Confirming usage habits (they haven't used it)

---

## Five Testing Tactics

### 1. Fake Door Tests

Put a "Get Early Access" or "Request Demo" button in your prototype. Track clicks.

**What it tells you:** Interest level, which features attract attention
**How to do it:** Add a button that goes to a simple email capture form

### 2. User Walkthroughs

Watch real target users interact with your prototype. Don't help them.

**What it tells you:** Confusion points, mental model mismatches, unexpected behaviors
**How to do it:**
1. Find 5 people who match your persona
2. Give them a task ("Show me how you'd check your runway")
3. Ask them to think aloud
4. **Don't help.** Silence is data.

### 3. Landing Page + Demo

Create a simple value proposition page that links to your prototype. Run $50 in ads.

**What it tells you:** Whether your positioning attracts the right people
**How to do it:**
1. Build a one-page landing describing the value
2. Add a "See Demo" button linking to your prototype
3. Run targeted ads to your persona
4. Track: Click-through rate, time on demo, email signups

### 4. Investor Signaling

Use the prototype in investor conversations to prove execution ability.

**What it tells you:** Whether you can turn ideas into artifacts quickly
**How to do it:** Build → show → gauge reaction. "I built this yesterday" is powerful.

### 5. Five-Second Test

Show someone your main screen for 5 seconds. Ask: "What was this for?"

**What it tells you:** Whether your value prop is immediately clear
**How to do it:** Use [usabilityhub.com](https://usabilityhub.com) or just do it in person

## What Feedback Actually Matters

### Signal (Pay Attention)

**Behavior statements:**
> "I would check this every Monday morning."
> "I'd open this when I'm doing payroll."

**Emotional reactions:**
> "Seeing that number would stress me out."
> "This would make me feel more in control."

**Alternative descriptions:**
> "Right now I do this in a spreadsheet."
> "I have to log into three different tools to see this."

### Noise (Usually Ignore)

**Feature suggestions:**
> "You should add X."
> "It would be cool if it could also do Y."

**Hypotheticals:**
> "I think people would love this."
> "My friend might use this."

**Polite encouragement:**
> "This is really cool!"
> "Great job!"

---

## The Kill Decision

**If 5 target users don't care → kill it or pivot.**

Signs they don't care:
- Lukewarm reactions ("That's interesting")
- No behavior commitment ("I might use this")
- Immediate feature requests (they're solving a different problem)
- Confusion about the core value

Don't polish failures. Move to the next idea.

---

## Running a User Walkthrough

### Before
1. Find 5 people who match your persona (not friends or family)
2. Prepare 3-5 tasks for them to complete
3. Set up screen recording if remote

### During
1. **Don't explain** — "I'm going to show you something. Tell me what you think it is."
2. **Ask them to think aloud** — "Tell me what you're thinking as you explore"
3. **Don't help** — If they're confused, that's data. Stay quiet.
4. **Ask behavior questions** — "Would you actually use this? When? How often?"

### After
1. Thank them (gift card or coffee if possible)
2. Write down observations immediately
3. Look for patterns across all 5 sessions

### Questions to Ask
- "What do you think this is for?"
- "Who do you think this is built for?"
- "What would you do first?"
- "What's confusing?"
- "Would you use this? When?"
- "What would make you not use this?"

---

## Quick Reference: What to Look For

| Signal Type | Good Sign | Bad Sign |
|-------------|-----------|----------|
| **Clarity** | "Oh, this shows me X" | "I'm not sure what this does" |
| **Relevance** | "I deal with this problem weekly" | "I don't really have this issue" |
| **Behavior** | "I'd check this Monday mornings" | "I might look at this sometimes" |
| **Emotion** | "This would give me peace of mind" | "It's fine, I guess" |
| **Urgency** | "Can I use this now?" | "Maybe someday" |
